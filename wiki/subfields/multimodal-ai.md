# Multimodal AI

Systems that process and generate across more than one modality — typically combinations of text, images, audio, and video within a single model.

**Why it matters** — the world is multimodal; humans communicate with language, images, and sound simultaneously. A model that bridges modalities can caption images, answer visual questions, generate images from text, and understand speech all in one system.

**Architecture** — most systems use a [[transformer|Transformer]] backbone with modality-specific encoders. CLIP (vision-language contrastive learning) was a landmark step. Frontier models ([[gpt|GPT-4V]], [[gemini|Gemini]], [[claude|Claude]]) accept and produce combinations of text, images, and audio.

**Model links** — [[dalle|DALL·E]] and [[stable-diffusion|Stable Diffusion]] (text→image); Whisper ([[openai|OpenAI]], speech→text); Gemini (text + image + audio natively).

**Related subfields** — [[nlp|NLP]], [[computer-vision|Computer Vision]], [[speech|Speech]].
