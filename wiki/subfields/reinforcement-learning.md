# Reinforcement Learning (RL)

A learning paradigm where an agent learns by acting in an environment, receiving rewards or penalties, and optimizing for long-term cumulative reward. Unlike supervised learning, no labeled examples are provided — feedback comes from interaction.

**Core concepts** — policy (the agent's decision rule), value function (expected future reward), exploration vs. exploitation tradeoff, Markov Decision Processes (MDPs).

**Key methods** — Q-learning, policy gradient methods, Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO), model-based RL.

**High-profile applications** — game playing (AlphaGo, Atari), robotics control, and crucially **[[rlhf|RLHF]]** (RL from Human Feedback), how modern [[llm|LLMs]] are aligned to be helpful.

**Key figures** — [[rich-sutton|Rich Sutton]] (co-developer of TD learning; standard RL textbook). AlphaGo at [[google-deepmind|Google DeepMind]] was a landmark RL result.
