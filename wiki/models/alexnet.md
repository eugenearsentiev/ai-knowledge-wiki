# AlexNet

The deep [[cnn|convolutional neural network]] that won the 2012 ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC) by a wide margin, launching the modern [[deep-learning|deep learning]] era. Created by Alex Krizhevsky, [[ilya-sutskever|Ilya Sutskever]], and [[geoffrey-hinton|Geoffrey Hinton]] at the University of Toronto.

**What it showed** — deep CNNs trained on GPUs with large datasets dramatically outperform prior hand-engineered methods. The gap was so large (15.3% vs. 26.2% top-5 error) it redirected the field almost overnight.

**Technical details** — 8 layers (5 convolutional, 3 fully connected), ~60M parameters, trained on two GPUs, used ReLU activations and dropout for regularization.

**Historical significance** — validated the ideas that [[geoffrey-hinton|Hinton]], [[yann-lecun|LeCun]], and [[yoshua-bengio|Bengio]] had championed through the lean years. Directly supports [[bitter-lesson|The Bitter Lesson]]: scale + compute + learned representations beat hand-crafted features.
