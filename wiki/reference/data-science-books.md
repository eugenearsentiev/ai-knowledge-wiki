# Data Science Reading List

Curated books for the data science and ML practitioner, grouped by purpose.

*Source: `data-science-books.md`*

## Foundations (Statistical Learning)
- **An Introduction to Statistical Learning** (James et al.) — most common entry point; free online; R and Python editions.
- **The Elements of Statistical Learning** (Hastie et al.) — heavier math; the canonical theory reference.
- **Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning** (Bishop) — rigorous; strong Bayesian/probabilistic framing.

## Practical / Hands-On
- **Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras, and TensorFlow** (Géron) — best all-around practical ML book.
- **Python for Data Analysis** (McKinney) — the pandas manual, by its creator.
- **Data Science from Scratch** (Grus) — builds tools from first principles in Python.

## Deep Learning
- **Deep Learning** (Goodfellow, [[yoshua-bengio|Bengio]], Courville) — standard theoretical reference.
- **Deep Learning with Python** (Chollet) — practical; by the Keras creator.

## Statistics
- **Practical Statistics for Data Scientists** (Bruce et al.)
- **Think Stats** / **Think Bayes** (Downey) — short, code-driven introductions.
- **Naked Statistics** (Wheelan) — non-technical intuition builder.

## Craft & Career
- **Storytelling with Data** (Knaflic) — data visualization and communication.
- **The Art of Statistics** (Spiegelhalter) — statistical reasoning and interpretation.

## Suggested beginner path
ISLR + Géron's *Hands-On ML* covers most of what an entry-level data scientist needs.
