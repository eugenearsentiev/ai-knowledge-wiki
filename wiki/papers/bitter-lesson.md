# The Bitter Lesson (2019)

A short essay by [[rich-sutton|Rich Sutton]], published on his blog in March 2019. One of the most widely cited and debated short pieces in AI.

**Core argument** — reviewing 70 years of AI research, Sutton observes that methods which leverage increased computation consistently and dramatically outperform methods that incorporate hand-built human domain knowledge. Search and learning are general; everything else is local optimization that eventually loses to scale.

**"Bitter"** — it's bitter because it implies that much of the domain-knowledge engineering researchers invested in was ultimately a dead end. The lesson has to be learned again and again, each time a new scaling result surprises the field.

**Implication for practitioners** — invest in general methods (search, learning, gradient descent at scale) rather than specialized knowledge architectures. This retrospectively explains [[alexnet-imagenet|AlexNet]], the [[transformer|Transformer]]'s dominance, and the LLM scaling era.
