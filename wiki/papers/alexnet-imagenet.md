# AlexNet / ImageNet Paper (2012)

"ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks" — Krizhevsky, [[ilya-sutskever|Sutskever]], [[geoffrey-hinton|Hinton]] — NeurIPS 2012. Often called simply "the AlexNet paper."

**What happened** — AlexNet won the 2012 ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge with a top-5 error of 15.3%, vs. 26.2% for the next-best entry. The margin was so large it redirected the field almost overnight.

**Technical contributions** — deep [[cnn|CNN]] architecture (5 convolutional + 3 fully connected layers, ~60M parameters), ReLU activations (much faster training than sigmoid/tanh), dropout regularization, multi-GPU training, large-scale data augmentation.

**Historical significance** — validated the ideas [[geoffrey-hinton|Hinton]], [[yann-lecun|LeCun]], and [[yoshua-bengio|Bengio]] had championed through the lean years. The starting gun of the modern [[deep-learning|deep learning]] era. A key data point for [[bitter-lesson|The Bitter Lesson]].
