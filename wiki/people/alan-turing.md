# Alan Turing

British mathematician and logician (1912–1954). Foundational figure for both theoretical computer science and AI.

**Key contributions** — the Turing machine (theoretical model of computation, 1936), the **Turing Test** (proposed in "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," 1950, as an operational criterion for machine intelligence), codebreaking work at Bletchley Park (WWII Enigma), foundational computability and complexity theory.

**The Turing Test** — Turing framed "can machines think?" operationally: if a machine can converse indistinguishably from a human, call it intelligent. Influential as a concept, widely discussed in philosophy of mind; modern AI practitioners treat it as a historical artifact rather than a useful benchmark.

**Legacy** — the **Turing Award** (ACM, given annually) is named after him — the "Nobel Prize of computing."
