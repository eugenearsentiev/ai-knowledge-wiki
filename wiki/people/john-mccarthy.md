# John McCarthy

American computer scientist (1927–2011). Coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" and organized the 1956 Dartmouth Workshop where the field was formally founded.

**Key contributions** — invented the Lisp programming language (1958, one of the oldest high-level languages still in use), foundational work on symbolic reasoning and knowledge representation, the Advice Taker (1959, early vision of a general reasoning program), and coining "AI" as a field name.

**Dartmouth Workshop (1956)** — McCarthy organized this workshop with Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon, and others. The event is considered the founding moment of AI as a named discipline. McCarthy's core belief: every feature of intelligence can be described precisely enough for a machine to simulate it.

**Legacy** — central figure of the [[history/timeline|symbolic AI era]]. The field he named evolved far beyond the symbolic methods he championed.
