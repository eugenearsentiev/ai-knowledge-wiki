# Geoffrey Hinton

British-Canadian cognitive psychologist and computer scientist. Often called the "Godfather of Deep Learning." Retired from Google in 2023, citing concerns about AI risks and the need to speak freely about them.

**Key contributions** — popularized backpropagation (with Rumelhart and Williams, 1986), Boltzmann machines, dropout regularization, pioneering work on [[cnn|CNNs]] with [[yann-lecun|LeCun]], and co-authored the [[alexnet-imagenet|AlexNet]] paper (2012) with Krizhevsky and [[ilya-sutskever|Sutskever]].

**Recognition** — 2018 Turing Award (shared with [[yann-lecun|LeCun]] and [[yoshua-bengio|Bengio]]) for foundational contributions to [[deep-learning|deep learning]].

**Institutional** — professor at University of Toronto; long-time Google Brain researcher; co-founder of DN Research (acquired by Google 2013).

**Legacy** — AlexNet's 2012 ImageNet victory is widely considered the starting gun of the modern deep learning era.
