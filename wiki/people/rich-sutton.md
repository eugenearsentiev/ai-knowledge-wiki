# Rich Sutton

Canadian computer scientist; professor at the University of Alberta. One of the founders of modern [[reinforcement-learning|Reinforcement Learning]].

**Key contributions** — co-developed temporal-difference (TD) learning, Q-learning foundations, and the Actor-Critic framework. Co-authored the standard RL textbook with Andrew Barto: *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction* (freely available online).

**"The Bitter Lesson"** — a short 2019 essay arguing that the history of AI shows general methods leveraging computation consistently outperform methods that incorporate human knowledge. Widely cited; the clearest short statement of why the deep learning / scaling era happened.

**Influence** — his RL work underpins modern applications including game playing (AlphaGo uses RL concepts he helped develop) and [[rlhf|RLHF]] (which adapts RL for LLM alignment).
