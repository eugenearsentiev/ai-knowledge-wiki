# History of AI — Timeline

A chronological backbone. Key eras and turning points that explain why the field looks the way it does today.

**1940s–1950s — Origins.** [[alan-turing|Alan Turing]]'s "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" (1950) proposed the Turing Test. The **Dartmouth Workshop** (1956) — organized by [[john-mccarthy|John McCarthy]], Marvin Minsky, and others — coined "Artificial Intelligence." Early optimism ran high.

**1960s–1980s — Symbolic AI / GOFAI.** Intelligence as manipulation of logical rules. **Expert systems** encoded specialist knowledge as rule sets — commercially useful but brittle. Two **AI winters** (mid-1970s, late 1980s) followed inflated expectations.

**1990s–2000s — Statistical turn.** Learning from data replaced hand-written rules. SVMs, decision trees, ensembles (→ [[classical-ml|Classical ML]]). IBM's Deep Blue beat Kasparov (1997).

**2012 — Deep learning revolution.** [[alexnet-imagenet|AlexNet]] won ImageNet by a wide margin. [[geoffrey-hinton|Hinton]], [[yann-lecun|LeCun]], [[yoshua-bengio|Bengio]] — champions of neural networks through the lean years — later received the Turing Award.

**2017 — [[attention-is-all-you-need|"Attention Is All You Need."]]** The [[transformer|Transformer]] architecture introduced by Google. Foundation for BERT, GPT, and essentially all modern large models.

**2022 — ChatGPT.** Public inflection point. [[generative-ai|Generative AI]] reaches mass audiences; frontier-model race begins.

**Recurring lesson** — [[bitter-lesson|The Bitter Lesson]]: general methods that scale with compute beat hand-crafted approaches.
